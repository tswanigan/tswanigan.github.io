    1. What is Crochet? Who Can Do It? Is This a Hobby For Me?

    Learning any new hobby can feel intimidating in the beginning. Everything is new, terminology, time invested, money, many factors. A hobby is something you love to do. When people ask me about my hobbies, I always say they are my escape away from reality, something to wash away the stresses life can sometimes bring. However, stressing about learning a new hobby is the last thing we want. Luckily you are here and I will help you learn to crochet the easy, stress-free way! If you are still on the fence if this hobby for you, let's first dive into what crochet really is.

    Crochet is a yarn craft that consists of only a few basic materials. The good news is that crochet is a pretty inexpensive hobby! When you become more advanced you’ll find yourself at crafting stores frequently… that can add up! To start a project, all you need is a crochet hook and a skein of yarn. We will talk more on this terminology in the ‘materials’ section. Some people confuse crochet with knitting, but they are in fact different. Knitting uses two knitting needles, crochet uses one hook- a whole lot easier! All in all, when you are crocheting, you will be working up a project with your fiber (yarn type), one stitch at a time. The hook part of crochet is straightforward, you put your hook into the previous row you started. In as little as a few hours or even a couple months, you can have your own beanie, washcloth, blanket, etc. in your fingertips.

    1. Materials/What You Need/Where to?

    As stated before, you really only need two things to begin your first crochet project- a hook and yarn. The most you should spend for your first project is around $8! I will note, however, there are additional materials you can purchase when working with more difficult projects. Some of these things include a hook organizer and a few other knicks and knacks that can make crocheting a little easier.

    First we can start with the part that interests most people, Yarn! Yarn is the most fun of all the crochet supplies you’ll need because of all the colors, fibers, and weights it comes in. Most new crocheters start with a low cost acrylic yarn, such as Lion Brand. It can be easily found in most craft stores. It holds up quite well, comes in a variety of colors, and as stated, is very affordable. You can use acrylic yarn for hats, scarfs, and winter-ish projects! Wool is another alternative-slightly more expensive. Cotton yarn (my new personal favorite) is another route to venture when purchasing. If you are looking to make clothes for the summer or boho-chic hats, this is the yarn for you. Yarn comes in a number of weights too. The weight of the yarn refers to the thickness of the yarn. It’s a range from super-fine to super-bulky. I linked a picture for better understanding.


    Next, the last piece you need to start your first project, the Hook! The hook is an essential piece to starting your project, they too, come in a number of different sizes. I recommend a 6.5 crochet hook when starting off. You can refer to the picture as well for a reference. When researching be sure to check if US or UK terms are used.


    Lastly, you may question where you can find yarn and crochet hooks, the answer is quite a few places. I prefer Joanns and Michaels, but Amazon, Walmart, and many other places sell supplies that you may need or want. Scissors ARE helpful!
